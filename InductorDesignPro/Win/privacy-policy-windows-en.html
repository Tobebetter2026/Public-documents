Privacy Policy (Windows Edition)

Applicable to the Windows edition of "Inductor Design Pro" distributed via Microsoft Store

This Privacy Policy (hereinafter "Policy") applies to the Windows edition of the "Inductor Design Pro" application distributed via Microsoft Store (hereinafter "Application," "we," or "us"). This Policy explains how we handle information related to your device. Please read it before using.

1. Introduction

"Inductor Design Pro" is a local computation and design assistance tool for power inductors used in Buck, Boost, Forward, and interleaved Buck (multi-phase) topologies. The interface offers Chinese and English display; language preference is primarily determined by your device's system language setting and does not change the data handling principles described in this Policy. We follow the principle of privacy-first, with computation and data primarily completed locally on your device: your design content and custom database are by default stored only on your device; unless otherwise stated below, we do not collect personally identifiable information for advertising or third-party sharing.

2. How We Collect and Use Information

2.1 Basic Principles
This application does not upload your design schemes, specification parameters, or "My Database" contents to the developer's servers; we cannot remotely read such data from your device.

2.2 Information Processed on Your Device
Data you input or generate includes but is not limited to:

  • Electrical specifications and design constraints (voltage, current, frequency, topology and mode, etc.)
  • Core selection, iteration results, and fields included in summary reports
  • Locally saved design projects and interface preferences
  • Custom materials, cores, and bobbins in "My Database"

PDF reports and JSON design files you generate on your device are created locally; if you share them via system sharing, email, or other applications, they are handled by your chosen recipient according to their terms.

The above information is used solely to present calculation results and export files on your device and is not uploaded to the developer's servers.

2.3 Analytics and Diagnostics (Anonymous)
We may learn of device model, system version, and aggregated usage information through standard mechanisms provided by Microsoft (such as Microsoft Store analytics and anonymous crash diagnostics), without including your design details. You can manage diagnostic data sharing in Windows Settings → Privacy & security → Diagnostics & feedback to limit such reporting.

3. Data Storage

Design data is primarily stored in the application's local data folder on your device. Uninstalling the application or clearing its data will delete local content according to system behavior. This application does not use iCloud or any other cloud backup service to store your design data; if Windows includes the application's local data in a system-level backup you have configured (e.g., File History), that is governed by the relevant Microsoft service's own terms, and we cannot access such backups.

This application does not store a local trial start time as the sole basis of trust, and does not unlock paid features solely from a local timestamp. The 7-day full-feature trial is calculated locally on-device starting from first launch, independent of whether you ultimately choose an annual subscription or a one-time buyout. Purchase records, receipts, and (for the annual subscription) auto-renewal are all handled by Microsoft (Microsoft Store / Partner Center). The application reads valid subscription or purchase status via the official Windows.Services.Store API when needed, and may cache that status and its expiration time locally (the one-time buyout is permanent and has no expiration) for interface display and basic state continuity while offline.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell design data. In accordance with application distribution, payment, and system service requirements, Microsoft (Microsoft Store, Partner Center, subscription and purchase records) and network communication recipients you actively choose (such as the hosting provider of a privacy policy page opened in your browser) handle minimally necessary information related to their respective connections according to their own terms.

5. Third Parties and Application Web Pages

If you open instructional or privacy policy pages provided with the application in your browser, static content is loaded over HTTPS and does not contain embedded advertising scripts that track you personally. External links such as GitHub Issues are accessed at your own discretion via your browser.

6. Children's Privacy

This application is not designed for children under 13 years of age; we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

7. Your Control

You can manage design schemes and your database within the application; uninstalling the application deletes on-device data. Auto-renewal settings for the annual subscription are managed by you in Windows Settings → Apps → Subscriptions or the Microsoft Store "Subscriptions" page; the one-time buyout is a permanent unlock and requires no renewal management. Purchase records and receipts are handled by Microsoft (Microsoft Store) according to its own terms.

8. Policy Changes

We may update this Policy; material changes will be communicated through Microsoft Store release notes or an appropriate in-application notice. Continued use constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.

10. Windows Platform Notes

This application is distributed via the Microsoft Store as a packaged desktop application (MSIX), built on a WebView2-based local interface. Regardless of which Windows device you use, the data handling principles remain the same: design and calculation take place on your current device, and this product does not transmit your design file contents to the developer.

The application requires no location, camera, microphone, or other unnecessary permissions to use core functionality; core iteration and loss calculation can be completed offline. Opening the Microsoft Store subscription/purchase page, verifying purchase status, or browsing web help requires network connectivity.

On first launch, Windows may perform standard application integrity checks (such as Microsoft Defender SmartScreen) as part of the platform's official security mechanism, which may require network connectivity; thereafter, the application can run offline. Design projects are stored in the application's local data folder and are not shared with other applications.

The 7-day full-feature trial, the annual subscription, and the one-time buyout are all completed via Microsoft (including Microsoft Store subscription and purchase rules, as shown in-application and in the Microsoft Store), and we do not use subscription/purchase information for third-party advertising profiling.
